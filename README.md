- 3D Scene ZIP folder (full Visual Studio project)
- Design Decisions document

---

## Reflection

### How do I approach designing software?

When designing software, I focus on breaking the problem into smaller components first. For this project, I thought about the scene as individual objects (desk, laptop, floor, lighting) rather than one large system. This made it easier to plan and implement each part step by step.

I also try to think from a user perspective, especially with camera movement and navigation. If something feels confusing or unnatural to use, I adjust the design.

---

### What new design skills has your work on the project helped you to craft?

This project helped me improve my ability to design spatial environments and think in 3D. I learned how to position objects relative to each other, apply textures, and use lighting to make a scene feel realistic.

I also developed a better understanding of how small design choices (like lighting angle or texture detail) can significantly impact the final result.

---

### What design process did you follow for your project work?

I followed an iterative process. I started with basic shapes, then added transformations, textures, and finally lighting. Each step built on the previous one.

Instead of trying to make everything perfect at once, I focused on getting something working first, then improving it over time.

---

### How could tactics from your design approach be applied in future work?

Breaking problems into smaller parts and iterating is something I can apply to any software project. This approach is especially useful in larger systems where complexity can quickly become overwhelming.

---

### How do I approach developing programs?

I approach development by getting a basic version working first, then refining it. I prioritize functionality before optimization or polish.

I also rely heavily on testing small changes frequently, which helps prevent larger issues later on.

---

### What new development strategies did you use while working on your 3D scene?

One key strategy I used was incremental development. Instead of implementing everything at once, I added features like movement, textures, and lighting one at a time.

I also improved my debugging skills, especially when dealing with rendering issues and object positioning.

---

### How did iteration factor into your development?

Iteration was a major part of this project. I repeatedly adjusted object placement, camera controls, and lighting until the scene looked and behaved the way I wanted.

Each milestone helped refine the previous work rather than starting from scratch.

---

### How has your approach to developing code evolved throughout the milestones?

At the beginning, I focused more on just getting things to work. By the end, I was more intentional about structure, readability, and making incremental improvements.

I also became more comfortable working with graphics-specific concepts like transformations and rendering pipelines.

---

### How can computer science help me in reaching my goals?

Computer science provides the foundation for building real-world systems and solving complex problems. Skills like problem decomposition, debugging, and system design are directly applicable to my career as a software engineer.

---

### How do computational graphics and visualizations give you new knowledge and skills for your education?

This project helped me understand how visual systems work at a lower level. Concepts like coordinate systems, transformations, and lighting models are important for many areas of computer science, including simulations and game development.

---

### How do computational graphics and visualizations give you new knowledge and skills for your career?

In a professional setting, these skills can be applied to areas like frontend development, data visualization, and interactive applications.

Even outside of graphics-heavy roles, understanding how systems render and display information improves my ability to build better user experiences.
